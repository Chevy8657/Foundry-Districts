# Foundry Constitution (Business)

Note: Notion is the canonical, authoritative source for the Foundry Constitution. This file is a repository-backed copy for versioning, review, and traceability. Any changes to the canonical doctrine in Notion must be recorded there and mirrored here via the Notion → repo export workflow.

## Foundry Constitutional Principle #1

Foundry is governed by doctrine, proven by evidence, computed by deterministic rules, interpreted by intelligence, and approved by humans.

## Purpose
This document defines the immutable business doctrine of Foundry. It intentionally avoids any engineering or implementation detail (languages, runtimes, frameworks). It should remain stable across rewrites and technology changes.

## What Foundry Is
- An Operational Intelligence Platform that transforms verified operational evidence into executive decisions.
- A system that computes and presents verified operational truth — not a generator of truth.

## Core Tenets
- Evidence before interpretation.
- Creator ≠ Verifier (segregation of duties).
- Every executive metric must trace to evidence.
- Version everything (doctrine, rules, evidence, metrics).
- Never invent operational truth.
- Never overwrite historical records.
- Keep business doctrine separate from implementation.

## Primary Concepts (examples)
- Operational Truth: Verified outcomes derived from auditable evidence.
- Executive Operating Review™: The canonical executive report derived from Verified Truth.
- Verified Hours: Time entries or operational records that have been verified.
- Critical Path: The set of evidence and dependencies that materially affect executive decisions.
- Governance & Change Control: Human-led workflows that authorize changes to doctrine, rules, and operational practice.

## Governance
- Notion holds the canonical doctrine and decision log.
- Human governance approves any changes to doctrine.
- All doctrine changes must include rationale, decision owner, and linked audit evidence.

## Traceability Requirements
- Every executive metric or recommendation must be traceable to specific evidence items and the rule version used to compute it.
- Rule versions referenced must point to an immutable artifact (rule commit or Notion revision).

## Where This Lives
